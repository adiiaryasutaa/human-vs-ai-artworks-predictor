How it works

Three steps

  1. 01

    Submit an artwork

    A painting, drawing, or digital piece — by file, link, clipboard, or camera. PNG, JPG, or WEBP.

  2. 02

    The model looks

    An EfficientNetV2 classifier studies the artwork and its mirror, averaging both reads for a steadier judgment.

  3. 03

    You get a verdict

    Human, AI, or Not sure — with a confidence reading on the verdict meter.

Under the hood

An honest read, not a verdict carved in stone

Model
An EfficientNetV2 classifier trained to tell human-made art from AI-generated art.
Made for
Artwork — paintings, drawings, and digital art. It isn't tuned for photographs or screenshots, so verdicts on those are unreliable.
Mirror averaging
Each artwork is read alongside its horizontal flip and the two scores are averaged — small changes in framing sway the result less.
The grey zone
When confidence lands between 40% and 60%, it says Not sure rather than guess.
Limits
Generators unlike its training data, and heavily re-compressed art, can still fool it. Treat results as likely, not proof.
